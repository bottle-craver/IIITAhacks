Many of us think about, what will be the career opportunities after B.tech ? Everyone has this question in their minds but none get a answer which satisfy them completely. Actually, there are several career opportunities after B.Tech as one can go for the further higher studies or can also opt for a job. Other options are PSUs, MNCs, IAS/PCS etc.

After completing B.Tech, there are two main options-
Go for higher studies as M.Tech, MBA etc
Go for a job(Private or Government)
Since after B.Tech, one can be mature enough to take their own decision
as per his interest. If your economically very strong, then you can compete in the exams like:

Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering(GATE)- GATE Exam is national level exam which is conducted for the admission in the M.Tech Courses of the top Indian Institutes.
Common Admission Test(CAT)- CAT Exam is conducted by the IIMS for admission in MBA Program of various IIMs in India.
Graduate Record Examination(GRE)- GRE Exam is conducted for admission in the Foreign Universities in M.S. Courses.
Graduate Management Admission Test(GMAT)- GMAT Exam is conducted for admissions in MBA Program globally.
Management Aptitude Test(MAT)- MAT Exam is conducted for the admission in various B- Schools.
Union Public Service Commission(UPSC)- UPSC Exams are conducted for the IAS/PCS Exams as well as other such level exams.
Test of English as a Foreign Language(TOEFL)- TOEFL Exam is conducted to check your English language which is must for the studies in Foreign Countries.

Indian Engineering Services (IES): Indian Engineering Services (IES) is for those canditates who desire a career in most reputed government departments like Indian Railways, Military Engineering Services, Central Engineering Services, Telecommunication Department, Central Water Services etc.For entering Engineering Services, a candidate has to appear in an Engineering Services Examination conducted by the UPSC every year in the month of May/June.

Public Sector Units (PSUs): PSU are a government owned corporations, state-owned companies, state enterprises, publicly-owned corporations or government business enterprises. Public Sector Enterprises (like NTPC, ONGC, IOCL, BPCL, HPCL, BARC, DRDO etc), a aspirant is required to have good technical knowledge, apt general apt and reasoning and a pleasing personality.PSUs recruit through individual entrance Examinations having technical and non technical questions.

As for the second option, if you are interested in taking a job, then you are eligible enough after B.Tech to find a job for you. One can get the jobs in various fields like:
Software Industries
Hardware Industries
Journalism
Management
IT Industries and others too.
Along with these, there are also Government Jobs in the Public Sectors where one can apply and after clearing the exam, can get a job which will have more job security as compared to the private one.These all things depends only on the person, either he can go for the Master degree for strong hold over his learning or can try his gained skill in the Job. These all Career Opportunities after B.Tech help you to enhance your gained skill as well as prepare you for a more competitive field ahead. There several Career Opportunities after B.Tech like these which are mentioned above.
For Jobs, there are the options of On-Campus as well as Off-Campus opportunities to get a job.

Thus, we can conclude that there are several career opportunities after B.tech for the aspirant which he can follow as per his interest and comfort. But its all depend on them, where they want to be.
